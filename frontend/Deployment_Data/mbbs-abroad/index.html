Introduction to MBBS Abroad

Every year, thousands of medical aspirants plan to study medicine abroad to fulfill their dream of becoming successful doctors. But exactly what is MBBS abroad, and why do Indian students choose foreign medical universities?

MBBS abroad for Indian students refers to pursuing an undergraduate medical degree (MD Physician or MBBS) outside India. Due to limited government seats, high NEET competition, and massive donation demands in private Indian medical colleges, choosing an international destination is the smartest alternative. Reputed foreign medical universities offer high-quality, fully English-medium medical programs that are globally recognized, giving students a pathway to practice medicine anywhere in the world.

MBBS Abroad Eligibility Criteria 2026

The National Medical Commission (NMC) has set strict guidelines for Indian students planning to study MBBS abroad after NEET. To be eligible, you must meet the following:

NEET QualificationIt is mandatory to qualify the NEET-UG examination.
Age CriteriaThe student must be at least 17 years old by December 31st of the admission year.
Academic PCB PercentageMinimum 50% aggregate marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (PCB) in 12th standard.
Valid PassportA valid regular Indian passport is required for university application and visa processing.

Best Country for MBBS Abroad for Indian Students

MBBS in Kyrgyzstan

Kyrgyzstan is known for providing highly affordable MBBS abroad options with top NMC recognized universities like Osh State University, Jalal Abad State University (JASU), and Central Asian International Medical University (CAIMU).

  • Fees: Approx. 15-20 Lakhs (Total)
  • Duration: 5 Years
  • Recognition: WHO, NMC, FAIMER
  • Indian Support: Indian mess, 1000+ students

MBBS in Russia

Russia has been a traditional favorite for foreign medical colleges for Indian students due to its historic, century-old federal medical universities and strong clinical base.

  • Fees: Approx. 20-35 Lakhs (Total)
  • Duration: 6 Years
  • Recognition: WHO, NMC, ECFMG
  • Indian Support: High FMGE passing rate

MBBS in Kazakhstan

Kazakhstan is emerging as a premier destination with completely English medium programs spanning 5 years, providing an ideal mix of affordability and excellent education.

  • Fees: Approx. 20-25 Lakhs (Total)
  • Duration: 5 Years
  • Recognition: WHO, NMC
  • Indian Support: Direct flights, safe

MBBS in Georgia

Georgia offers premium European-standard medical education. It is considered one of the safest countries globally with 100% English medium medical faculties.

  • Fees: Approx. 25-40 Lakhs (Total)
  • Duration: 6 Years
  • Recognition: WHO, NMC, WFME
  • Indian Support: Large Indian community

NMC Approved Medical Universities Abroad

Always ensure you apply to NMC Approved Medical Universities Abroad and WHO Approved Medical Universities. Studying at recognized institutions is critical because it allows you to appear for the FMGE/NExT screening test in India, keeps your degree valid internationally for USMLE/PLAB, and ensures the curriculum meets the 54-month study + 12-month internship NMC guidelines.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is MBBS abroad valid in India?

Yes, MBBS abroad is completely valid in India provided you complete your degree from an NMC and WHO approved medical university, study in English medium, and clear the NExT/FMGE exam upon returning to India.

Can I study MBBS abroad without NEET?

No, qualifying the NEET-UG exam is mandatory for Indian students who wish to study MBBS abroad and practice medicine in India later. You must have a valid NEET scorecard.

Which country is cheapest for MBBS abroad?

Kyrgyzstan and Kazakhstan are considered among the cheapest countries for MBBS abroad, offering high-quality medical education starting from INR 15 Lakhs to 20 Lakhs for the complete tuition fees.

What is the total cost of MBBS abroad?

The total cost of studying MBBS abroad, including tuition fees, hostel, and living expenses, generally ranges from INR 15 Lakhs to INR 35 Lakhs depending on the country and the university you choose.

Is MBBS abroad better than private colleges in India?

For many students, studying MBBS abroad is a better option because it offers affordable fees without any donations, global exposure, and modern infrastructure compared to high-cost private medical colleges in India.

Can Indian students practice after MBBS abroad?

Absolutely. After completing their MBBS abroad from recognized universities, Indian students need to clear the National Exit Test (NExT) or FMGE to obtain a license and practice medicine anywhere in India.
