What is MBBS Abroad?

MBBS abroad refers to the pursuit of an undergraduate medical degree (usually named MD Physician or MBBS) outside of India. Over the past decade, studying medicine abroad has emerged as the most popular and viable alternative for Indian medical aspirants who face stiff competition, high NEET cut-offs, and astronomical fee structures in private medical colleges in India.

International medical degrees obtained from top government and private colleges in countries like Kyrgyzstan, Russia, Georgia, and Kazakhstan are widely accredited by premier global education councils. By selecting a WHO and NMC recognized university, Indian medical graduates become eligible to practice internationally, including sitting for the licensing exam (NExT/FMGE) in India.

Why Indian Students Choose MBBS Abroad

With over 24 Lakh students appearing for NEET UG in India and only approximately 1 Lakh MBBS seats available (nearly half of which are in high-fee private colleges), tens of thousands of deserving students miss the opportunity to study medicine. Studying MBBS abroad solves this supply-demand gap by offering:

Affordable Tuition Fees

Tuition fees start at just INR 2.5 Lakhs to 4 Lakhs per year, with no donation or hidden capitation charges.

Global Recognition

Degrees are recognized globally by the World Health Organization (WHO), FAIMER, and the National Medical Commission (NMC).

English Medium Instruction

The entire 5-year or 6-year MBBS curriculum is taught fully in English, eliminating major language barriers.

State-of-the-Art Infrastructure

Top government universities abroad offer advanced simulative learning, vast clinical rotations, and historic anatomy labs.

MBBS Abroad Eligibility & NEET Requirements

To apply for an MBBS course in any country abroad, candidates must satisfy the basic eligibility criteria prescribed by the National Medical Commission:

  • Age Limit: The candidate must be at least 17 years old by December 31st of the admission year.
  • Academic Background: Must have passed 12th standard (Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and English) from a recognized board (CBSE, ICSE, or State board) with at least 50% marks for General category (40% for SC/ST/OBC).
  • NEET Requirement: Indian students must qualify for the NEET-UG exam in the current year or the previous two years to ensure eligibility to practice in India.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is NEET qualification mandatory for studying MBBS abroad?

Yes, as per the National Medical Commission (NMC) guidelines, qualifying the NEET-UG exam in the year of admission or during the previous two years is mandatory for Indian students who wish to practice medicine in India after graduation.

Are medical degrees from foreign universities valid in India?

Yes, medical degrees are valid in India provided the university is recognized by the WHO and the NMC, the medium of instruction is fully English, the course duration is at least 54 months with a 12-month internship, and you qualify for the FMGE/NExT exam in India.

What is the average cost of studying MBBS abroad?

The average cost ranges from INR 15 Lakhs to INR 45 Lakhs for the entire course, depending on the country and university. Kyrgyzstan offers one of the most affordable options starting at INR 15-20 Lakhs including hostel and food.

Will I get Indian food in international hostels?

Yes, in top student destinations like Kyrgyzstan, universities have dedicated Indian messes providing hygienic veg and non-veg Indian food, run by professional Indian chefs.

What is the NExT exam?

The National Exit Test (NExT) is a licensing and postgraduate placement exam in India that will replace the FMGE and NEET-PG. All MBBS graduates, whether from India or abroad, must qualify for NExT to register and practice medicine in India.
