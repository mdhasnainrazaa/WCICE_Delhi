Is MBBS in Georgia Valid in India?

Parents often worry about the validity of foreign medical degrees. Rest assured, Georgia strictly complies with all NMC foreign medical graduate rules, making the degree fully valid in India.

  • Course Duration: The course is exactly 54 months of study plus a 1-year clinical internship.
  • Clinical Internship: A mandatory 12-month internship is conducted within Georgia.
  • English Medium Requirement: The entire course syllabus is taught safely in English.
  • Licensing Pathway: Graduates can directly appear for the NExT/FMGE examination in India.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Georgia good for MBBS for Indian students?

Yes, Georgia offers a premium European medical education environment. It is extremely safe, highly reputed, and degrees are internationally recognized by the WHO and NMC.

Is NEET mandatory for MBBS in Georgia?

Yes, qualifying the NEET-UG examination is legally mandatory for all Indian students seeking admission to any foreign medical university.

What is the duration of MBBS in Georgia?

The duration of the MBBS program in Georgia is strictly 6 years, which includes 5 years of theoretical/clinical study followed by a 1-year mandatory clinical internship.

Is MBBS in Georgia taught in English?

Yes, all the major universities in Georgia offering medical programs for international students provide 100% English medium medical education.

What is the cost of studying MBBS in Georgia?

Studying MBBS in Georgia is slightly more premium than Central Asia, typically ranging from INR 30 Lakhs to 50 Lakhs total, depending heavily on the specific university and city.

Are Georgia medical universities approved by NMC?

Yes, the top medical universities in Georgia fully comply with the latest NMC guidelines and are listed in the World Directory of Medical Schools (WDOMS).

Does WCIEC help with Georgia visa documentation?

Absolutely. WCIEC Delhi provides end-to-end support including secure university applications, meticulous visa documentation, interview prep, and pre-departure briefings.
